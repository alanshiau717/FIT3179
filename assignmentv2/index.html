Crime In San Fransico - 2016

San Francisco is the 16th most populated city in the United States and the 4th in California. It is also the city with the highest salaries and house prices in the world mainly due to silicon valley being situated there. However, despite this, San Francisco also has one of the highest crime rates in all of America. The San Fransico Department is subdivded into 10 seperate police districts responsible for their respective areas.

Crime by Times

Looking at this visualisation, it can be clearly seen that crimes are mostly committe in the afternoon around 6pm. There is also a spike in crime rates at lunch time for all days.

Crime rates appear to be quite similar throughout the year. One exception being Christmas Day (Dec 25) where there is a large fall in crime and also New Years Day (1 Jan) where there is a large spike in crime rates.

The most popular day to commit a crime is during Friday and Saturday.

Common Crimes

Larceny/Theft is one of the biggest issued faced by San Fransico Police. It accounts for over 30% of all crime committed in San Fransico. Depending on the district, the second most popular crime is either Other Crimes or Non-Criminal Incidents. Non-Crimnal Incidents are offenses which aren't considered crimes. An exmple of this is minor speeding offenses.

Police Departments

In San Francisco crimes are quite evenly spread out across the different police departments. The deparment with the highest incidents is the Southern Deparment which oversees SoMa, South Beach and Ricon Hill.

In San Francisco the majority of cases are unresolved with some police departments having a much higher resolution rate compared to others.
